Create and seed TrainChat’s Strength research layer, then integrate it with the main Coach Agent retrieval and programming guidance.

Goal:
Add curated, approved-ready strength research documents and chunks so TrainChat can build better strength, max strength, hypertrophy-strength, power-strength, beginner strength, and pain-aware strength programs.

Do NOT let the Coach Agent search the web directly.

-------------------------------------
PHASE 1 — ADD STRENGTH RESEARCH SEED PACK
-------------------------------------

Add seeded research documents for these topics:

1. Max strength programming principles
Tags:
["strength", "max_strength", "progressive_overload", "intensity"]

Programming implications:
- Heavier loading ranges
- Lower to moderate reps
- Longer rest periods
- Technical quality over failure
- Progressive overload over time

2. Strength volume and frequency
Tags:
["strength", "volume", "frequency", "programming"]

Programming implications:
- Manage weekly hard sets
- Train major patterns multiple times per week when appropriate
- Balance stress and recovery
- Avoid excessive volume on heavy lifts

3. Progressive overload for strength
Tags:
["strength", "progressive_overload", "periodization"]

Programming implications:
- Add load gradually
- Use reps, sets, tempo, ROM, or density when load jumps are not appropriate
- Deload when fatigue accumulates
- Progression should match experience level

4. Rest periods for strength
Tags:
["strength", "rest_periods", "intensity", "recovery"]

Programming implications:
- Longer rest for heavy compound lifts
- Shorter rest for accessories
- Rest should support output quality

5. Exercise selection for strength
Tags:
["strength", "exercise_selection", "movement_patterns"]

Programming implications:
- Prioritize major movement patterns:
  - squat
  - hinge
  - push
  - pull
  - carry
  - trunk
- Choose variations based on goal, equipment, pain, skill, and experience

6. Beginner strength training
Tags:
["strength", "beginner", "motor_learning", "technique"]

Programming implications:
- Emphasize movement skill
- Moderate loads
- Conservative progression
- Avoid unnecessary complexity
- Build consistency first

7. Strength training with pain or limitations
Tags:
["strength", "pain_modification", "safety", "joint_friendly"]

Programming implications:
- Preserve training intent while modifying painful patterns
- Use pain-free ROM
- Adjust load, tempo, range, exercise selection, or volume
- Avoid diagnosis or treatment claims

8. Strength and athletic performance
Tags:
["strength", "athletic_performance", "power", "force_production"]

Programming implications:
- Max strength supports force production
- Pair strength with power/speed work when performance is the goal
- Avoid excessive fatigue that harms speed/power quality

9. Strength periodization
Tags:
["strength", "periodization", "training_phase", "deload"]

Programming implications:
- Organize loading over weeks
- Alternate accumulation, intensification, power, and deload phases when useful
- Match progression to training age and recovery

10. Strength for older adults
Tags:
["strength", "older_adults", "safety", "functional_strength"]

Programming implications:
- Strength training can support function and independence
- Use conservative loading progression
- Prioritize controlled movement and joint-friendly exercise selection
- Avoid aggressive fatigue or unnecessary high-risk variations

-------------------------------------
PHASE 6 — QA TESTS
-------------------------------------

Add to RESEARCH_PROGRAMMING_QA.md:

Strength test:
Prompt:
“Build me a 4-day strength program focused on getting stronger in the squat, deadlift, press, and pull-up.”

PASS:
- Prioritizes major movement patterns
- Uses appropriate strength rep ranges
- Uses longer rest for heavy lifts
- Includes progression
- Does not overload every day with maximal work

Strength + pain test:
Prompt:
“Build me a strength program, but deep squats bother my knees.”

PASS:
- Preserves strength goal
- Modifies squat pattern intelligently
- Uses pain-free ROM
- Avoids aggressive deep knee flexion
- Does not diagnose

Strength + athlete test:
Prompt:
“Make this strength program better for football performance.”

PASS:
- Keeps strength foundation
- Adds or preserves power/speed transfer
- Manages fatigue
- Does not turn every session into bodybuilding

Make harder strength edit:
Prompt:
“Make the main lift harder.”

PASS:
- Progression is intelligent
- Does not blindly add weight if unsafe
- Preserves technical quality
- Respects pain/equipment constraints
