Run a full audit of TrainChat’s Research Librarian Agent and research database activity.

Goal:
Understand what the Librarian Agent has processed, what research it added to the database, whether the research is useful, and whether it is actively helping Coach/Architect responses.

Audit only.

document candidate → librarian evaluation → chunk generation → research_documents / chunks tables → retrieval layer → Coach Agent prompt context.

- total documents processed
- total documents accepted
- total rejected/skipped
- total chunks generated
- documents by category
- documents by trustLevel
- documents by recommendation
- newest and oldest processed documents
- activity by day since the Librarian was created

- source

4. Quality audit
Review accepted research and flag:
- duplicate documents
- low-quality sources
- missing citations/source URLs
- vague titles
- overly broad chunks
- outdated findings
- unsafe medical/rehab claims
- content not relevant to strength, speed, mobility, recovery, pain, or programming

Trace how Coach Agent retrieves research.

Identify:
- retrieval function/file
- search/scoring method
- filters used
- category matching
- chunk limit
- whether trustLevel/confidence affects ranking
- whether warningFlags are excluded or surfaced
- whether research context is injected into build/edit/refinement prompts
