PHASE 1 — ADD SPEED RESEARCH SEED PACK
-------------------------------------

Add seeded research documents for these topics:

1. Sprint acceleration principles
Tags:
["speed", "sprint", "acceleration", "football", "athletic_performance"]

Programming implications:
- Short sprint distances
- Full recovery between reps
- High intent
- Low fatigue
- Horizontal force emphasis
- Technique quality over conditioning fatigue

2. Max velocity sprinting
Tags:
["speed", "max_velocity", "sprint_mechanics", "athletic_performance"]

Programming implications:
- Upright mechanics
- High-speed exposure
- Low total volume initially
- Adequate rest
- Quality over quantity

3. Plyometric training dosage
Tags:
["plyometrics", "power", "reactive_strength", "speed"]

Programming implications:
- Dose jumps based on experience
- Prioritize landing quality
- Avoid excessive contacts
- Progress from low to high intensity

4. Change of direction and deceleration
Tags:
["change_of_direction", "deceleration", "agility", "football"]

Programming implications:
- Deceleration strength
- Braking mechanics
- Cutting angles
- Progressive complexity
- Avoid random “ladder-only” agility programming

5. Strength-speed relationship
Tags:
["strength", "power", "speed", "force_velocity"]

Programming implications:
- Max strength supports force production
- Power training bridges strength to speed
- Heavy lifts should not create excessive fatigue before sprint work

6. Speed training fatigue management
Tags:
["speed", "recovery", "fatigue_management", "sprint"]

Programming implications:
- Long rest intervals
- Avoid speed work under heavy fatigue
- Separate high-output sprint work from high-fatigue conditioning when possible

-------------------------------------
PHASE 2 — ADD MOBILITY RESEARCH SEED PACK
-------------------------------------

Add seeded research documents for these topics:

1. Mobility vs flexibility
Tags:
["mobility", "flexibility", "range_of_motion", "movement_quality"]

Programming implications:
- Mobility = usable range under control
- Flexibility alone does not equal movement capacity
- Pair range-of-motion work with control/stability

2. Dynamic warm-ups and movement prep
Tags:
["warmup", "dynamic_warmup", "movement_prep", "mobility"]

Programming implications:
- Dynamic warm-ups before training
- Movement-specific prep
- Gradual ramp-up of intensity
- Static stretching is not the default pre-power choice

3. Hip mobility for athletes
Tags:
["hip_mobility", "mobility", "athletes", "sprint"]

Programming implications:
- Hip extension/flexion control supports sprinting and lower-body movement
- Use active mobility, positional strength, and controlled ranges

4. Ankle mobility and lower-body mechanics
Tags:
["ankle_mobility", "mobility", "squat", "deceleration"]

Programming implications:
- Ankle dorsiflexion affects squat, landing, and deceleration mechanics
- Use controlled mobility and progressive loading

5. Thoracic mobility and upper-body mechanics
Tags:
["thoracic_mobility", "mobility", "rotation", "upper_body"]

Programming implications:
- Thoracic rotation/extension can support overhead, rotational, and sprint posture mechanics
- Pair mobility with trunk control

6. Mobility and injury risk — conservative framing
Tags:
["mobility", "injury_risk", "movement_quality", "safety"]

Programming implications:
- Avoid claiming mobility prevents injuries by itself
- Frame mobility as one component of movement quality, readiness, and tolerance
- Do not make medical claims
