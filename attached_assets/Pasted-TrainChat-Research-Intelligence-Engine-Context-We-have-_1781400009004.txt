TrainChat Research Intelligence Engine

Context

We have already built:

- Product Intelligence Directory
- Exercise Intelligence Layer
- Performance Intelligence Engine
- Training Method Selection Engine
- Adaptation Forecasting System

I do NOT want to continue prioritizing product expansion.

The next major objective is to transform TrainChat from a product directory into a research-backed performance intelligence platform.

The goal is to create a Research Intelligence Engine that helps TrainChat reason from scientific evidence when selecting training methods, forecasting adaptations, prioritizing performance qualities, and explaining exercise selection.

Desired reasoning flow:

Assessment + Goal + Sport + Constraints
↓
Performance Profile
↓
Research Intelligence Layer
↓
Training Method Selection
↓
Exercise Selection
↓
Adaptation Forecast

NOT:

Goal
↓
Exercise List

PHASE 2 — TRAINING METHOD EVIDENCE GRAPH
==================================================

Create a Training Method Evidence Graph.

Structure:

Training Method
↓
Supported Adaptations
↓
Evidence Strength
↓
Population Support
↓
Expected Timeline
↓
Confidence Score

Example:

{
  method: "Resisted Sprint Training",

  supportedAdaptations: [
    "Acceleration",
    "Horizontal Force Production"

  confidence: 92,

  timelineWeeks: {
    minimum: 4,
    expected: 8,
    optimal: 12
  }
}

- Resisted Sprint Training
- Assisted Sprint Training
- Plyometrics
- Depth Jumps
- Bounding
- Heavy Strength Training
- Olympic Lifting
- Ballistic Training
- Medicine Ball Training

The system should reason about adaptations rather than exercises.

==================================================
PHASE 3 — POPULATION TRANSFER ENGINE
==================================================

Research does not transfer equally across populations.

Build a Population Transfer Engine.

Example:

{
  sourcePopulation: "College Volleyball Athletes",

  transferScores: {
    volleyball: 100,
    basketball: 90,
    soccer: 75,
    baseball: 40
  }
}

TrainChat should understand:

- Direct support
- Strong transfer
- Moderate transfer
- Weak transfer

Supported populations:

- Youth
- High School
- College
- Professional
- Male
- Female
- Team Sport
- Individual Sport
- Tactical
- General Fitness

When research does not directly match the athlete profile, TrainChat should estimate transfer confidence instead of assuming universal applicability.

==================================================
PHASE 5 — RESEARCH-BASED EXERCISE JUSTIFICATIONS
==================================================

Build a Research Justification Engine.

Example output:

{
  exercise: "Heavy Sled Sprint",

  primaryReason:
    "Selected to improve horizontal force production and acceleration.",

  evidenceSupport:
    "Strong evidence in field sport athletes.",

  confidence: 91
}

Every exercise recommendation should be explainable through:

- Performance need
- Adaptation target
- Research support
- Confidence score
- Population relevance

Avoid generic AI explanations.

Justifications should be generated from structured evidence.

==================================================
PHASE 6 — RESEARCH CONTRADICTION ENGINE
==================================================

Research often contains disagreement.

Build support for contradictory findings.

Example:

{
  topic: "Nordic Hamstrings",

  strongEvidence: [
    {
      adaptation: "Hamstring Injury Reduction",
      confidence: 95
    }
  ],

  mixedEvidence: [
    {
      adaptation: "Sprint Speed",
      confidence: 60
    }
  ]
}

Supported evidence categories:

- Strong Evidence
- Moderate Evidence
- Mixed Evidence
- Emerging Evidence
- Insufficient Evidence

TrainChat should communicate uncertainty rather than pretending all evidence is unanimous.

==================================================
SUCCESS CRITERIA
==================================================

TrainChat should be able to answer:

- Why was this training method selected?
- What adaptation does it target?
- How strong is the supporting evidence?
- How transferable is the evidence to this athlete?
- How long should adaptation realistically take?
- Where is the evidence mixed or uncertain?
- What level of confidence should we have in this recommendation?

The goal is NOT to build a research database.

The goal is to build a Research Intelligence Layer that makes program design, exercise selection, and adaptation forecasting more evidence-aware, defensible, and scalable.

Focus on architecture, reasoning systems, confidence models, adaptation logic, and future scalability.

Avoid building a simple citation library.

TrainChat should become capable of reasoning from evidence, not merely storing evidence.
