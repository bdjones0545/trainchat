Hypertrophy rules by exercise role:

1. Primary compound lifts
Examples: squat, trap bar deadlift, bench, press, rows, RDL.
Prescription:
- 3–5 sets
- 6–10 reps
- 90–180 sec rest
- Keep technique quality high
- Do not turn max-strength lifts into sloppy high-rep fatigue work

2. Secondary compound lifts
Prescription:
- 3–4 sets
- 8–12 reps
- 60–120 sec rest

3. Accessories / auxiliary
Prescription:
- 2–4 sets
- 10–20 reps
- 45–90 sec rest
- These are the main place to increase volume

4. Core / trunk
Do not treat all core as bodybuilding work.
Prescription:
- Anti-rotation / anti-extension: 8–15 reps or 20–45 sec holds
- Loaded trunk flexion/carry variations: moderate volume
- Preserve trunk function

5. Power exercises
Do not turn jumps, sprints, throws, or explosive lifts into high-rep hypertrophy work.
Options:
- Preserve low-rep power prescription
- Or swap to a safer hypertrophy-compatible exercise only if the session goal allows it

6. Mobility / prep
Do not convert mobility/prep into hypertrophy work.
Keep as prep, activation, or movement quality.

Hypertrophy safety verifier:
- If every exercise becomes 10–20 reps, repair.
- If jumps/sprints/throws become high-rep hypertrophy, repair.
- If core/prep/mobility gets converted into generic hypertrophy sets, repair.
- If accessories do not receive any increased volume during hypertrophy refinement, flag for review.

Acceptance test:
Click “More Muscle” / “Hypertrophy Emphasis” / “Increase Volume.”
Expected:
- Main lifts shift to 6–10 reps.
- Secondary lifts shift to 8–12 reps.
- Accessories shift to 10–20 reps or gain volume.
- Core stays trunk-appropriate.
- Power and mobility work preserve their role.
- Visible sets/reps/rest update correctly.
