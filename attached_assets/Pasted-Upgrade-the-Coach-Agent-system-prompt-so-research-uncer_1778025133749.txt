Upgrade the Coach Agent system prompt so research uncertainty and warning flags are reflected in user-facing coaching responses.

Goal:
When retrieved research includes caution notes or warning flags, the Coach should communicate uncertainty appropriately instead of presenting guidance as absolute fact.

Do not redesign retrieval.
Do not change Librarian scoring.
Do not alter research injection structure.
This is a Coach-response behavior upgrade only.

Implementation

1. Add a new section to the Coach system prompt in ai.ts

Section title:
"EVIDENCE CONFIDENCE & UNCERTAINTY HANDLING"

Rules:

When retrieved research includes:
- caution notes
- conflicting_evidence
- expert_consensus
- old_evidence
- single_study
- lacks_replication
- population_mismatch
- medical_claim_risk
- overclaim_risk

The Coach must:
- communicate appropriate uncertainty
- avoid absolute claims
- avoid overstating effectiveness
- avoid presenting debated findings as settled science

The Coach should:
- qualify guidance naturally
- maintain coach voice
- stay practical and confident without sounding uncertain or robotic

2. Add approved language examples

Preferred phrases:
- “generally effective”
- “in most cases”
- “often works well”
- “current evidence suggests”
- “typically helpful”
- “depends on recovery capacity”
- “varies between athletes”
- “should be adjusted based on response”
- “use conservatively at first”
- “monitor how you respond”

Avoid:
- “this definitely works”
- “guaranteed”
- “always”
- “best for everyone”
- “scientifically proven” (unless extremely high confidence)

3. Add evidence confidence translation behavior

If warningFlags include:
- conflicting_evidence
  → Coach should mention variability or debate naturally.

Example:
“Concurrent strength and conditioning work is generally effective, but the interference effect varies depending on recovery, intensity, and session structure.”

- old_evidence
  → Coach may reference foundational principles without overstating certainty.

Example:
“These are foundational sprint principles that still hold up well, although newer monitoring methods now exist.”

- single_study
  → Coach should avoid overgeneralization.

Example:
“This approach showed promising results in smaller studies, but individual response can vary.”

- medical_claim_risk
  → Coach must avoid diagnostic/treatment framing.

Example:
“This may help reduce irritation and improve tolerance to training, but it’s not a substitute for medical evaluation.”

4. Maintain strong coach tone

Important:
The Coach should still sound:
- confident
- practical
- actionable
- performance-oriented

Do NOT:
- become academic
- cite papers directly unless explicitly asked
- overwhelm users with disclaimers
- sound hesitant or robotic

5. Apply to all relevant paths

This behavior should apply during:
- new program builds
- edits/refinements
- mobility/pain adjustments
- recovery guidance
- conditioning recommendations
- speed/power guidance
- return-to-training discussions

6. Final QA

Test retrieval-backed responses for:
- hypertrophy
- concurrent training
- tendon pain
- ACL return-to-training
- sprint force-velocity profiling

Expected:
- Coach naturally reflects evidence confidence level
- warningFlags subtly influence tone
- responses remain practical and high-trust
- no robotic disclaimer spam
