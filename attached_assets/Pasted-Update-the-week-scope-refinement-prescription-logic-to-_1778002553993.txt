Power rules by exercise role:

1. True power exercises
Examples: jumps, bounds, med ball throws, Olympic lift derivatives, accelerations, sprints, low-load explosive lifts.
Prescription:
- 2–5 sets
- 2–5 reps
- 60–180 sec rest depending on intensity
- Keep quality high
- Never convert to high-rep endurance work

2. Primary strength lifts shifted toward power
Examples: squat, trap bar deadlift, bench, press, hinge.
Prescription:
- 2–4 sets
- 2–5 reps
- moderate load moved fast
- 2–3 min rest
- Add coaching cue: “move with intent / bar speed”

3. Secondary compound lifts
Prescription:
- 2–4 sets
- 3–6 reps
- 90–150 sec rest
- Keep explosive intent, but do not overload fatigue

4. Accessories / auxiliary
Do NOT turn accessories into heavy power work.
Prescription:
- Keep supportive hypertrophy/strength-support range
- Usually 8–15 reps
- 45–90 sec rest
- Add power-support cue only when appropriate

5. Core / trunk
Do NOT force 2–5 reps unless it is truly a power trunk drill.
Prescription:
- Anti-rotation / anti-extension: 8–15 reps or 20–45 sec holds
- Explosive trunk/med ball work: 2–5 sets of 3–6 reps
- Preserve trunk function

6. Mobility / prep
Do not convert to power prescriptions.
Keep prep/mobility intent.

Power safety verifier:
- If transformation is power/explosive and every exercise becomes 2–5 reps, repair.
- If core/accessory/mobility exercises are converted into primary power prescriptions, repair.
- If true jumps/sprints/throws become high-rep endurance prescriptions, repair.
- If power work has very short rest that would degrade output quality, repair.
