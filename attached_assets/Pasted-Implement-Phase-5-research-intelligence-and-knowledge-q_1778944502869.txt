Implement Phase 5 research intelligence and knowledge quality improvements for TrainChat.

Do NOT add generic AI frameworks.
Do NOT add Firecrawl, LangChain, CrewAI, Claude Code, or autonomous browsing systems.
Do NOT expose raw research ingestion directly to users.
Do NOT replace the current Research Librarian architecture.

Goal:
Improve research quality, retrieval precision, source trust weighting, recency handling, coaching applicability, and long-term maintainability of TrainChat’s research intelligence layer.

Preserve the current:
- admin-curated research pipeline
- deterministic coaching architecture
- Research Librarian role
- coaching-first philosophy

========================
PHASE 5 PRIORITIES
========================

1. Research retrieval scoring improvements
Audit current research retrieval scoring.

Tasks:
- Improve retrieval ranking using:
  - relevance
  - trust level
  - recency
  - coaching applicability
  - evidence quality
- Weakly-related research should not enter prompts.
- Prefer fewer high-quality chunks over many weak chunks.
- Maintain deterministic filtering where possible.

Suggested scoring dimensions:
- tag relevance
- title relevance
- semantic overlap
- trustLevel weighting
- publication recency weighting
- applicability to athlete/coaching context
- specificity vs genericity

Do not overcomplicate.

2. Recency awareness
Research from 2017 and 2025 should not be treated equally.

Tasks:
- Add recency weighting if publication date exists.
- More recent evidence should slightly outrank older evidence when quality is comparable.
- Do NOT blindly suppress older foundational papers.
- Add special handling for:
  - foundational/classic literature
  - evergreen physiology concepts
  - newer intervention studies
- If publication date is missing, fall back gracefully.

3. Research applicability classification
Not all evidence is equally useful for coaching decisions.

Tasks:
- Add or improve a lightweight classification layer:
  - foundational
  - applied coaching
  - mechanistic
  - emerging
  - weak/low applicability
- Coaching-oriented evidence should rank higher for direct program decisions.
- Mechanistic evidence can still support explanations.
- Preserve existing trustLevel structure if already present.

4. Research injection quality control
Audit how research enters prompts.

Tasks:
- Prevent low-scoring or loosely-related chunks from inflating prompt size.
- Add hard caps on:
  - number of injected chunks
  - total token footprint if practical
- Prefer the highest-signal chunks.
- Remove duplicate/redundant research snippets before injection.

5. Admin research review workflow improvements
Improve the admin-side review process only.

Tasks:
- Surface:
  - trust level
  - recency
  - applicability
  - retrieval score
  - tags/categories
- Make it easier to reject weak evidence.
- Preserve human approval workflow.
- Do not auto-promote research into coaching behavior.

6. Knowledge versioning / auditability
TrainChat should know where coaching knowledge came from.

Tasks:
- Add lightweight versioning or metadata tracking where practical.
- Track:
  - when research was added
  - who approved it
  - trust level
  - source category
  - major tags
- Do not overbuild a full academic database.
- Goal is traceability and future maintainability.

7. Coaching explanation quality
Use research intelligently without sounding robotic.

Tasks:
- When research influences a coaching explanation:
  - keep explanations coach-like
  - avoid academic jargon overload
  - avoid citation spam
- Use research to support:
  - progression logic
  - fatigue management
  - power sequencing
  - recovery rationale
  - injury-sensitive modifications
- Do not expose raw retrieval chunks to users.

8. Research retrieval observability
Add lightweight logging/observability.

Track:
- retrieval score
- selected chunks
- rejected chunks
- why chunks were rejected
- token usage estimate if practical

This can be logging only.
No dashboard required yet.

9. Guardrails against “research hallucination”
TrainChat should not invent evidence.

Tasks:
- Ensure unsupported claims are not framed as established science.
- Emerging/weak evidence should be framed cautiously.
- Avoid overconfident phrasing on uncertain findings.
- Preserve coaching practicality.

10. Research quality tests
Add focused tests where practical.

Test:
- relevance ranking
- recency weighting
- duplicate chunk removal
- weak chunk rejection
- applicability classification
- trustLevel weighting
- prompt injection caps
- foundational paper preservation
- emerging evidence caution language

========================
IMPORTANT CONSTRAINTS
========================

- Preserve the deterministic Architect + Coach separation.
- Preserve admin-controlled research ingestion.
- Do not build an autonomous research crawler.
- Do not add live web search into user coaching flows.
- Keep retrieval focused and high-signal.
- Favor coaching usefulness over academic complexity.
- Keep research invisible unless it improves coaching quality or trust.
